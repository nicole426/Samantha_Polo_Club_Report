UNCORKED — Why Wine Cellars Became South Florida's New Status Symbol

On March 27, 2026, a single bottle of 1945 Domaine de la Romanée-Conti sold at Acker's La Paulée auction in New York for $812,500 — a new world record. That number is the cleanest macro signal for what's happening at the top of the South Florida luxury market right now.

In Boca Raton, Delray Beach, and Palm Beach, the wine cellar has been pulled out of the basement, walled in glass, and parked on the main floor. The tier driving the conversations I'm having with $5M+ buyers and sellers is now $500,000 to $2,000,000+ on the cellar alone.

This week's brief covers what's actually being built at that tier, why the global auction market (Sotheby's $127.5M / Christie's Koch $28.8M / Acker $175M) is feeding it, the four South Florida firms doing the high-end work, and the resale math — when a cellar adds value, and the $500K mistake that quietly destroys it.
